THE DOG'S CURLY TAIL

August 28, 2013 0 comments

Once, a man went to a forest and saw a sage. He fell at the sage' s feet and asked a charm to solve all his problems. The sage told him a magic word and told him, "Repeat the magic word several times and a ghost would appear. It will do whatever you want,but, beware,if you don't give him work,he will kill you." The man thought that it was easy and sat at a place. He repeated the magic word serval times and ghost appeared. The man have the first job to build a place for him. Immediately a place appeared in front of him. Then,the man wanted lot money, jewels,gold etc., In a moment he got all of them. The ghost said, "I can't remain idle for a moment." The man then wanted a city in place of the forest. In a second the forest became a beautiful city. ￼ The man had no other work to give to the ghost. The gost ran behind the man and the man ran towards the sage. The sage looked at the poor man. He felt pity for the poor man. Then, the sage told the man a trick. The dog's tail. The ghost sat down and started to straighten the dog's tail. But the tail curled up. Again he tried and again it curled up. At last he said to the man, "sir, I compromise with you. I leave all that I gave you." The ghost vanished. Moral: The world is like a dog's curly tail. It can never be changed at one's will..

THE SEVEN JARS

August 28, 2013 3 comments

Many years ago, there was a barber. He was the king's barber. He lived with his wife in a small but. He was not happy. He wanted to be rich. One day he went to the forest. He sat under a tree and as usual began to think about money. Just then he heard a voice, "Do you want to ne rich? Do you want gold?" The barber said, "yes! Yes!" The voice asked, " How much?" The barber replied, "seven jars full." The voice said, "Alright go home and you will find them." The barber felt happy and rushed home. He and his wife were surprised to see the jars of gold. One by one he opened the jars, but the seventh jar was half empty. Both of them decided to fill the seventh jar with whatever gold they had. But still the jar was not full. They started saving money. They ate less and they tried to fill the seventh jar. One day the king observed that the barber was looking sad. He enquired and learnt that the barber was also offered the seven jars that he had been offered earlier. The king said that one day he himself went to the tree and was offered seven jars of gold. He observed that the seventh jar was half empty. So he went to the tree and spoke to the voice which said that the seventh jar would never get full. It was a triap. It was a ' jar of desire'. It will never get full. The moment the barber realized this fact all the seven jars in his house vanished; along with the jars, his hard earned money and gold. Moral: GREED BRINGS PAIN.

THE PUNDIT WHO COULD NOT SWIM

August 28, 2013 1 comment

Once,a pundit was crossing the Ganges in a boat all alone. He was feeling bored,so he started talking to the boatman The pundit was very proud of his knowledge. He said that he studied various books--the Vedas, the Vedanta,etc. He wanted to show his vast knowledge to the boatman.He asked him,"Do you know the Vedanta?" The Boatman humbly replied, "No,revered sir." Then, feeling proud the pundit asked, "ok, do you know the Ramayana and the Mahabharata?" The boatman again replied, "No, revered sir." Now, the pundit was feeling really proud of his knowledge. He once again asked the boatman, "Do you know any philosophy?" "No,revered sir" , said the boatman. Then, after some time, a great storm began and the boat and the boat was about to sink. The boatman said to the pundit. " Sir, the boat is about to sink. Can you swim?" "No" , replied the pundit. The boatman said, "I don't know any Vedas, the Vedanta Ramayana and Mahabharata, but I can swim and save my life. " The boatman then jumbed into the river. The pundit was left alone in the boat and drowned along the boat. Moral:Have practical knowledge..
